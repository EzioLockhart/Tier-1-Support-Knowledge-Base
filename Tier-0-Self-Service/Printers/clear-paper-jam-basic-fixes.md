## Fix 1: Clear a Paper Jam

**IMPORTANT:** Turn off the printer and unplug it before reaching inside. Printer components can be hot.

1. Turn off the printer using the power button
2. Unplug the power cord from the back of the printer
3. Open all accessible doors and covers:
   - Main front cover
   - Rear access door (if your printer has one)
   - Paper tray
4. Look for the stuck paper:
   - Check the paper path — where paper enters and exits
   - Use a flashlight if needed to see inside
5. Remove the paper carefully:
   - Pull the paper slowly and steadily in the direction of the paper path
   - Do not pull backwards against the paper path if possible
   - If the paper tears, remove every piece — even small bits can cause another jam
6. Check the paper tray:
   - Remove all paper from the tray
   - Fan the paper stack to separate sheets
   - Reload the paper neatly — do not overfill (stay below the maximum fill line)
7. Close all doors and covers securely
8. Plug the printer back in and turn it on
9. Try printing again

**If the jam persists:** Look for a "hidden" piece of paper. Check the back of the printer, behind the toner cartridge area, and under the paper tray.

## Fix 2: Printer Will Not Print At All

1. Check the basics first:
   - Is the printer turned on? Look for lights or a display screen
   - Is the power cord firmly plugged into the printer and wall outlet?
   - Is the USB or network cable connected?
2. Restart the printer:
   - Turn the printer off using the power button
   - Unplug the power cord and wait 60 seconds
   - Plug it back in and turn it on
3. Check for error messages on the printer display:
   - "Paper Jam" — go to Fix 1
   - "Low Ink" or "Replace Toner" — go to Fix 3
   - "Offline" — turn the printer off and on again
4. On your computer, check that the correct printer is selected:
   - Open the document you want to print
   - Click File > Print
   - Make sure your printer name is selected in the printer dropdown

## Fix 3: Print Quality Is Poor or Faded

1. Check ink or toner levels:
   - Look at the printer display screen for ink/toner status
   - On your computer: open printer settings and check levels
2. For inkjet printers:
   - If ink is low, replace the cartridge
   - If print quality is streaky, run the printer's built-in cleaning function:
     - Find "Maintenance" or "Tools" on the printer display
     - Select "Clean Printhead" or "Nozzle Check"
3. For laser printers:
   - If toner is low, replace the toner cartridge
   - Remove the toner cartridge and gently shake it side to side — this redistributes remaining toner
4. Check paper quality:
   - Use fresh, dry paper
   - Avoid paper that has been exposed to humidity
5. Check print settings on your computer:
   - In the print dialog, look for "Print Quality" or "Quality Settings"
   - Select "Normal" or "Best" instead of "Draft"

## Fix 4: Printer Says "Out of Paper" but Paper Is Loaded

1. Remove all paper from the tray
2. Check that the paper type is correct:
   - Plain paper for everyday printing
   - Photo paper only for photos
3. Fan the paper stack:
   - Hold the stack and flip through the edges to separate each sheet
   - This prevents sheets from sticking together
4. Reload the paper:
   - Align the paper guides snugly against the paper edges
   - Do not push the guides too tight — paper should slide freely
   - Make sure the paper stack is below the maximum fill line
5. Check if the paper tray is fully inserted:
   - Pull the tray out completely
   - Push it back in firmly until it clicks or stops

**Why this happens:** Paper sensors can fail to detect paper if sheets are stuck together, the tray is not fully inserted, or the paper guides are misaligned.

## Fix 5: Print Jobs Stuck in the Queue

1. On your computer, look for the printer icon in the system tray (bottom-right corner)
2. Double-click the printer icon to open the print queue
3. Click on each stuck document and click "Cancel" or press the Delete key
4. If documents will not cancel:
   - Restart your computer
   - The stuck jobs should clear automatically after restart
5. After clearing the queue, try printing a simple document like a text file or test page

**If this happens often:** Avoid printing large documents (50+ pages) in one job. Split them into smaller batches.

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- You cleared the paper jam but the printer still shows "Paper Jam" error
- The printer makes grinding or unusual noises
- Print quality is still poor after replacing ink or toner
- The printer shows an error code you do not understand
- Multiple people cannot print to the same printer
- You need a replacement ink cartridge or toner ordered

---

## Prevention Tips

- Use good quality paper — cheap or damp paper causes jams
- Store paper in a dry place — humidity makes paper stick together
- Do not overfill the paper tray — stay below the maximum fill line
- Replace ink or toner when the printer warns you, not after it runs out completely
- Keep printer covers closed to prevent dust from getting inside
