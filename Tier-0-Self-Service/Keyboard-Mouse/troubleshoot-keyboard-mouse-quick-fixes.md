## Fix 1: Wireless Keyboard or Mouse Not Working

**Symptom:** Device does not respond at all, or works intermittently.

**Step 1: Replace the batteries**
This is the most common fix for wireless devices.
1. Turn the keyboard or mouse over
2. Open the battery compartment (usually slides off or has a small latch)
3. Remove the old batteries
4. Insert fresh batteries — make sure the + and - ends match the markings
5. Close the compartment
6. Wait 5 seconds and test

**Step 2: Check the USB receiver**
The small USB receiver is what connects your wireless device to the computer.
1. Look for the small USB receiver plugged into your computer
2. Unplug it, wait 5 seconds, and plug it back in
3. Try a different USB port if possible
4. Some wireless devices have a **"Connect"** button on the bottom — press it after reconnecting the receiver

**Step 3: Turn the device off and on**
1. Look for a power switch on the bottom or side of the device
2. Switch it OFF, wait 5 seconds, then switch it ON
3. Some devices have a small LED that lights up when on

**What to tell yourself:** "Wireless devices need fresh batteries. If the batteries are old, the device may work intermittently or not at all. This is the fix 90% of the time."

## Fix 2: Bluetooth Device Keeps Disconnecting

**Symptom:** Device connects but drops out frequently.

**Step 1: Remove and re-pair the device**
1. Click Start > Settings (gear icon) > **"Bluetooth & devices"**
2. Find your keyboard or mouse in the list
3. Click the three dots (...) next to it > **"Remove device"** > **"Yes"**
4. Put your device into pairing mode (check the device manual — usually hold a button for 3-5 seconds)
5. Click **"Add device"** > **"Bluetooth"**
6. Select your device when it appears
7. Wait for it to connect

**Step 2: Check battery level**
1. Low battery causes Bluetooth disconnections
2. Go to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > find your device
3. Some devices show battery percentage here
4. If battery is low, replace or recharge

**Step 3: Move closer to the computer**
1. Bluetooth has a range of about 10 metres (30 feet)
2. Walls, metal objects, and other wireless devices can reduce range
3. Move the device closer to your computer

**What to tell yourself:** "Bluetooth devices can lose their pairing. Removing and re-pairing is like introducing two people again — it resets the connection."

## Fix 3: Wired Keyboard or Mouse Not Responding

**Symptom:** USB device plugged in but not working.

**Step 1: Try a different USB port**
1. Unplug the device from the current USB port
2. Plug it into a different USB port
3. Wait 10-15 seconds for your computer to recognize it
4. You should hear a short chime when the device is recognized

**Step 2: Avoid USB hubs**
1. If the device is plugged into a USB hub, unplug it
2. Plug directly into a USB port on the computer itself
3. USB hubs may not provide enough power for some devices

**Step 3: Check for physical damage**
1. Look at the USB connector — are any pins bent?
2. Check the cable for cuts, frays, or kinks
3. If the cable is damaged, the device may need replacement

**Step 4: Restart your computer**
1. If the device is still not working, restart the computer
2. Click Start > Power > Restart
3. Leave the device plugged in during restart

**What to tell yourself:** "USB ports can fail. The ports on the front of a desktop are often less reliable than the ones on the back. Try a different port first."

## Fix 4: Specific Keys Not Working

**Symptom:** Some keys work fine but others do not respond.

**Step 1: Clean under the keys**
1. Turn the keyboard upside down and gently shake to dislodge crumbs and dust
2. Use a can of compressed air to blow out debris (hold the can upright, short bursts)
3. Gently wipe the keys with a slightly damp cloth (not wet)

**Step 2: Check for stuck keys**
1. Press each non-working key slowly
2. Does it feel different from working keys? Does it stick down?
3. If a key is physically stuck, it may need professional cleaning

**Step 3: Try an external keyboard**
1. If you are using a laptop keyboard, plug in any USB keyboard
2. If the external keyboard works, your laptop keyboard may have a hardware issue

**What to tell yourself:** "Food crumbs, dust, and hair can get under keys and prevent them from pressing down fully. A quick clean often brings dead keys back to life."

## Fix 5: Keyboard Typing Wrong Characters

**Symptom:** Pressing a key types a different character than expected (e.g., @ instead of ").

**Step 1: Check keyboard language**
1. Look at the system tray (bottom-right corner) for a language icon (e.g., ENG)
2. Click it and make sure the correct language is selected
3. If you see multiple languages, the wrong one may be active

**Step 2: Remove unwanted keyboard layouts**
1. Click Start > Settings > **"Time & language"** > **"Language & region"**
2. Under your language, click the three dots > **"Language options"**
3. Under "Keyboards", remove any layouts you do not use
4. Keep only the one that matches your physical keyboard

**Step 3: Check Num Lock**
1. Look for the **Num Lock** key on your keyboard
2. If Num Lock is ON, some letter keys may type numbers instead (common on compact keyboards)
3. Press Num Lock once to toggle it off and test

**Common layout confusion:** US keyboards have @ above the 2 key. UK keyboards have " above the 2 key. If you are getting the wrong one, your keyboard layout setting is wrong.

## Fix 6: Mouse Cursor Jumping or Erratic

**Symptom:** Cursor moves unpredictably or jumps across the screen.

**Step 1: Clean the sensor**
1. Turn the mouse over
2. Look at the small light on the bottom — this is the sensor
3. Use a dry cotton swab to gently clean around the sensor
4. Remove any dust, hair, or debris

**Step 2: Change the surface**
1. Optical mice need a non-reflective surface
2. Try using a mousepad or a piece of paper under the mouse
3. Glass, mirrors, and glossy surfaces cause cursor jumping

**Step 3: Check for wireless interference**
1. Move other wireless devices away from the mouse (routers, phones, Bluetooth speakers)
2. If using a wireless mouse, move the USB receiver closer — use a USB extension cable if needed

**What to tell yourself:** "A single hair or speck of dust on the sensor can make the cursor jump wildly. Cleaning the sensor is the first thing to try."

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- You replaced batteries and the wireless device still does not work
- The keyboard or mouse has visible physical damage
- A wired device is not recognized on any USB port on any computer
- Keys are physically broken or missing
- The device is under warranty and needs replacement

## FAQ

**Q: How long should keyboard and mouse batteries last?**
**A:** Most wireless keyboards last 6-12 months on a set of batteries. Wireless mice typically last 3-6 months. Rechargeable devices need charging every few weeks.

**Q: Can I use any USB keyboard or mouse with my computer?**
**A:** Yes. Standard USB keyboards and mice are universal — any brand will work with any Windows computer. No special setup is required.

**Q: Why does my keyboard work in the login screen but not after I sign in?**
**A:** This usually means a program or setting is interfering with your keyboard after login. Try restarting in Safe Mode or contact IT support.
