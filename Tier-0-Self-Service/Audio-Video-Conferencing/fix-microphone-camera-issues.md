## Fix 1: People Cannot Hear Me

**Step 1: Check if you are muted**
This is the most common cause. Everyone does it.

- In Teams: Look for the microphone icon. If it has a **slash through it (🚫)**, you are muted. Click it to unmute.
- In Zoom: Look for the microphone icon in the bottom-left corner. If it is **red and has a slash**, you are muted. Click it to unmute.
- Keyboard shortcut for both: **Ctrl + Shift + M** toggles mute on and off.

**Step 2: Check the correct microphone is selected**
- Teams: Click **"..."** (more options) > **"Settings"** > **"Devices"** > Under "Microphone", select your device
- Zoom: Click the **arrow (^)** next to the microphone icon > select your device from the list

**Step 3: Test your microphone**
- Teams: Settings > Devices > **"Make a test call"** — follow the prompts
- Zoom: Settings > Audio > **"Test Mic"** — speak and see if the bar moves
- If the test call works, your microphone is fine — the issue may be meeting-specific

**Step 4: Check physical mute buttons**
- Look at your headset or webcam for a physical mute switch
- Some headsets mute when you raise the microphone boom
- Check the cable is fully plugged in

## Fix 2: I Cannot Hear Others

**Step 1: Check your speaker or headset volume**
- Look at the volume icon in the system tray (bottom-right corner)
- Click it and make sure the volume is not at zero
- Check your headset or speaker physical volume knob

**Step 2: Check the correct speaker is selected**
- Teams: Settings > Devices > Under "Speaker", select your device
- Zoom: Click the arrow (^) next to the microphone icon > "Audio Settings" > select your speaker

**Step 3: Test your speaker**
- Teams: Settings > Devices > "Make a test call"
- Zoom: Settings > Audio > "Test Speaker"
- If you hear the test sound, your speakers work

**Step 4: Check headset connection**
- If using Bluetooth, make sure the headset is connected and charged
- If using USB, try a different USB port
- If using the computer's built-in speakers, make sure nothing is plugged into the headphone jack

## Fix 3: My Camera Shows a Black Screen

**Step 1: Check the camera privacy shutter**
Many laptops and webcams have a physical privacy shutter or switch.
- Look at the top of your laptop screen for a small sliding cover
- Slide it to uncover the camera lens
- On external webcams, look for a lens cap or rotating ring

**Step 2: Turn on your camera in the meeting**
- Teams: Click the camera icon in the meeting toolbar. If it has a slash, your camera is off.
- Zoom: Click **"Start Video"** in the bottom-left corner.

**Step 3: Check which camera is selected**
- Teams: Settings > Devices > under "Camera", select your camera
- Zoom: Click the arrow (^) next to the camera icon > select your camera

**Step 4: Close other apps that use the camera**
- Close any other applications that might be using your camera (Skype, FaceTime, another meeting app)
- Only one application can use the camera at a time

**Step 5: Restart your computer**
- If the camera still shows black, restart the computer
- This clears camera conflicts between applications

## Fix 4: Microphone or Camera Not Found

If the app says "No microphone found" or "No camera detected":

**Step 1: Check the device is plugged in**
- For USB devices: unplug and plug into a different USB port
- For Bluetooth: go to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > check the device is connected
- Wait a few seconds for your computer to recognize the device

**Step 2: Check Windows privacy settings**
- Click Start > Settings > **"Privacy & Security"**
- Click **"Microphone"** in the list on the left
- Make sure **"Microphone access"** is turned ON
- Make sure your meeting app (Teams, Zoom, or your browser) is turned ON in the list
- Go back and click **"Camera"** — repeat the same checks

**Step 3: Restart the meeting app**
- Close Teams or Zoom completely (right-click the icon in the system tray > Quit or Exit)
- Reopen the app and try again

## Fix 5: Audio Sounds Bad or Robotic

**Step 1: Check your internet connection**
Poor audio quality is usually an internet issue, not a microphone issue.
- Move closer to your Wi-Fi router
- Ask others in your home to pause streaming or downloads during your call
- If possible, connect your computer directly to the router with an Ethernet cable

**Step 2: Turn off video temporarily**
- In Teams or Zoom, turn off your camera
- Video uses much more bandwidth than audio
- If audio improves with video off, your internet is the bottleneck

**Step 3: Use a headset**
- Built-in laptop microphones pick up room echo
- Using a headset (even basic earphones with a mic) dramatically improves audio quality

**Step 4: Close other applications**
- Close browser tabs, file downloads, and other programs
- Each one uses a small amount of internet and computer resources

## Quick Test Before Every Important Meeting

Do this 5-minute check before important meetings:

1. Open Teams or Zoom
2. Go to Settings > Devices
3. Select your correct microphone, speaker, and camera
4. Run a test call or test your microphone and speaker
5. Check your camera preview looks clear and you are well-lit
6. Ensure you are not muted

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- Your microphone or camera is not detected even after checking all settings
- Windows privacy settings are correct but apps still cannot access devices
- The device works in one app but not another
- You see a "Driver error" or "Device not working" message
- Physical damage to the microphone, camera, or cables

## FAQ

**Q: Why does my camera work in one app but not Teams or Zoom?**
**A:** Each app has its own device settings. Check that the correct camera is selected inside the specific app. Also check Windows privacy settings to make sure that specific app has permission.

**Q: Can I use my phone as a microphone for my computer?**
**A:** Not directly through Teams or Zoom. If you are having microphone issues, you can join the meeting from your phone while viewing content on your computer. This is a good temporary workaround.

**Q: Why do I sound like a robot to others?**
**A:** This is almost always an internet connection issue, not a microphone problem. Try moving closer to your Wi-Fi router, turning off your video, or using a wired Ethernet connection.
