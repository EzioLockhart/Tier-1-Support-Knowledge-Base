## Welcome to Your New Role

This checklist helps you set up all your IT systems on your first day. Work through each section in order. Most items take just a few minutes.

**Before you start, make sure you have:**
- Your username and temporary password (provided by HR or your manager)
- Your company email address
- Your smartphone (for MFA setup)

## Step 1: Log Into Your Computer

1. Turn on your computer
2. At the login screen, enter your **username**
   - Your username format may be: firstname.lastname or firstname.lastname@company.com
   - Check your welcome letter for the exact format
3. Enter your **temporary password** exactly as provided
   - Passwords are case-sensitive — check Caps Lock is OFF
4. Press Enter

**If login fails:**
- Make sure you are connected to the corporate network (in the office) or VPN (at home)
- Type your password into Notepad first to see it clearly, then copy it
- If you try more than 3 times, your account may lock — contact IT

---

## Step 2: Change Your Password

On first login, you will be prompted to create a new password.

1. Enter your temporary password again in the "Current password" field
2. Create a **new password** that meets these requirements:
   - At least 8 characters (your company may require more)
   - At least one uppercase letter (A-Z)
   - At least one lowercase letter (a-z)
   - At least one number (0-9)
   - At least one special character (! @ # $ %)
   - Cannot be a password you have used before
3. Enter the new password again to confirm
4. Press Enter

**Password tips:**
- Use a phrase you can remember: "FirstDay@Company2026!"
- Do not use personal information (birthday, name)
- Write it down temporarily if needed, then destroy the note

---

## Step 3: Set Up Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds security by requiring your phone to approve sign-ins.

**Quick setup:**
1. On your computer, go to: **https://aka.ms/mfasetup**
2. Sign in with your email and new password
3. Follow the prompts to set up the Microsoft Authenticator app
4. On your phone:
   - Download "Microsoft Authenticator" from the App Store or Google Play
   - Open the app and follow the setup steps
   - Scan the QR code shown on your computer screen
5. Approve the test notification

**Why this matters:** MFA protects your account from hackers. You will need your phone to sign in on new devices.

---

## Step 4: Set Up Email on Your Computer

**Option A: Use Outlook (Recommended)**
1. Click Start > type "Outlook" > open it
2. Outlook will detect your account automatically
3. Enter your email address and password
4. Wait for setup to complete — your emails will appear

**Option B: Use Webmail (No setup needed)**
1. Open a web browser
2. Go to: **outlook.office.com** or your company's webmail address
3. Sign in with your email and password
4. You can access email, calendar, and contacts here

---

## Step 5: Set Up Email on Your Phone

1. Download **Microsoft Outlook** from your phone's app store
2. Open the app and tap **"Add Account"**
3. Enter your work email address
4. Enter your password
5. Approve the MFA notification on your phone
6. Wait for emails to sync
7. Enable notifications so you see new emails

---

## Step 6: Connect to VPN (If Working Remotely)

VPN connects your home computer to the company network.

1. Click Start > type the name of your VPN client (ask IT if unsure)
2. Open the VPN application
3. Enter the server address (provided by IT)
4. Click **"Connect"**
5. Enter your username and password
6. Approve the MFA notification if prompted
7. The VPN icon should show "Connected"

**When to use VPN:** You need VPN to access shared drives, internal websites, and some applications. Email and Teams often work without VPN.

---

## Step 7: Access Shared Drives and Files

**Check for mapped drives:**
1. Open **File Explorer** (folder icon)
2. Look under "This PC" for drives like S:, Z:, or other letters
3. Double-click a drive to open it — you may be prompted for your password

**Access SharePoint or OneDrive:**
1. Open a web browser
2. Go to: **office.com** or your company's intranet
3. Sign in with your email
4. Look for SharePoint sites or OneDrive

---

## Step 8: Install Required Applications

Your manager or IT should provide a list of required apps. Common ones include:

**Microsoft Teams:**
1. Go to: **teams.microsoft.com**
2. Download the desktop app
3. Install and sign in with your email

**Web browser:**
- Microsoft Edge is pre-installed on Windows
- To install Chrome: google.com/chrome

**Other applications:**
- Check with your manager for department-specific software
- Some apps can be installed from the Company Portal (check Start menu)

---

## Step 9: Test Everything

Run through this final checklist:

- [ ] Can you log into your computer with your new password?
- [ ] Can you send and receive emails in Outlook?
- [ ] Can you access Teams and send a message?
- [ ] Can you access shared drives (S: drive, etc.)?
- [ ] Can you connect to VPN (if remote)?
- [ ] Does email work on your phone?
- [ ] Can you access your organization's intranet or SharePoint?

## Common First-Day Issues

| Problem | Quick Fix |
|---------|-----------|
| Can't log in | Check username format, check Caps Lock, try typing password in Notepad first |
| Account locked | You tried the wrong password too many times — contact IT to unlock |
| Email not working | Wait 1-2 hours — new mailboxes sometimes take time to activate |
| VPN won't connect | Check your internet first (open google.com). Restart the VPN program |
| Can't access shared drives | Make sure VPN is connected. The drives may need time to map |

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- Your account does not work after following this checklist
- You cannot log in after multiple attempts
- Required applications are missing
- You need access to specific systems or folders
- You have not received your username or temporary password

## FAQ

**Q: How long does it take for my accounts to be fully set up?**
**A:** Most accounts work immediately on your start date. Email and some applications may take 1-2 hours to fully activate. If something is still not working after 4 hours, contact IT.

**Q: Can I set up my work email on my personal phone?**
**A:** Yes. Use the Microsoft Outlook app. Your organization may require you to set up a PIN or passcode on your phone as a security measure.

**Q: What if I start before my official start date?**
**A:** Your IT accounts are usually activated on your official start date. If you start early, your accounts may not work yet. Contact your manager to coordinate with IT.
