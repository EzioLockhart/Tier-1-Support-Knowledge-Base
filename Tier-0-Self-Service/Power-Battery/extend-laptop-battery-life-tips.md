## Tip 1: Lower Your Screen Brightness

The screen uses more battery than anything else on your laptop.

**How to do it:**
1. Look for the brightness keys on your keyboard — usually marked with a **sun icon (☀️)**
2. Hold the **Fn** key and press the brightness down key repeatedly
3. Or click the battery icon in the system tray (bottom-right) and use the brightness slider
4. Aim for **50% brightness or lower** — every reduction saves significant battery

**Quick estimate:** Reducing brightness from 100% to 50% can nearly double your remaining battery time.

---

## Tip 2: Turn On Battery Saver

Windows has a built-in Battery Saver that automatically reduces background activity.

**How to do it:**
1. Click the **battery icon** in the system tray (bottom-right corner)
2. Move the slider to **"Battery saver"** or click the battery saver button
3. Or go to Settings > System > Power & battery > turn on **"Battery saver"**
4. Set it to turn on automatically when battery drops below a certain percentage

**What it does:**
- Dims the screen slightly
- Stops email and calendar background syncing
- Reduces notifications
- Limits background app activity

---

## Tip 3: Close Unused Applications and Browser Tabs

Every open program and browser tab uses power, even when you are not looking at it.

**What to close:**
1. **Browser tabs:** If you have more than 5-10 tabs open, close the ones you are not actively using
2. **Video conferencing:** Close Teams, Zoom, or Skype if you are not in a meeting
3. **Music and video:** Close Spotify, YouTube, Netflix, or any media apps
4. **Cloud sync:** Pause OneDrive, Google Drive, or Dropbox syncing
5. **Adobe apps:** Close Photoshop, Acrobat, or other heavy programs when not in use

**How to see what uses the most battery:**
1. Go to Settings > System > Power & battery > **"Battery usage"**
2. This shows which apps have used the most battery in the last 24 hours
3. Close or limit the top battery users when on battery power

---

## Tip 4: Turn Off Wi-Fi and Bluetooth When Not Needed

Your laptop constantly searches for networks and devices, using power even when idle.

**How to do it:**
1. **Wi-Fi:** Click the network icon in the system tray > click the Wi-Fi button to toggle it OFF
2. **Bluetooth:** Click the Bluetooth icon in the system tray > toggle it OFF
   - If you do not see it, go to Settings > Bluetooth & devices > toggle Bluetooth OFF
3. **Airplane mode:** The quickest way to disable all wireless — click the network icon > select "Airplane mode"

**When to turn these off:**
- Working on documents or spreadsheets offline
- Watching a downloaded movie
- On a long flight or train ride
- When you do not need internet

## Tip 5: Use the Right Power Mode

Windows has power modes that balance performance and battery life.

**How to change it:**
1. Click the **battery icon** in the system tray
2. Move the slider:
   - **"Best power efficiency"** — Maximum battery life, lower performance
   - **"Balanced"** — Good mix of battery and performance
   - **"Best performance"** — Maximum speed, shortest battery life
3. For the longest battery, select **"Best power efficiency"**

**When to use each mode:**
- On battery and need maximum time: Best power efficiency
- Plugged in and need speed: Best performance
- Normal use: Balanced

## Tip 6: Stop Background Apps

Some apps run in the background even when you close their windows.

**How to check and stop them:**
1. Go to Settings > Apps > Installed apps
2. Click the three dots (...) next to any app > **"Advanced options"**
3. Under "Background apps permissions", set to **"Never"** for apps that do not need to run in the background
4. Common background battery drainers:
   - Spotify, Apple Music, or other music apps
   - Skype or other communication apps
   - News and weather apps
   - Mail and calendar (if you do not need real-time notifications)

---

## Tip 7: Check Your Battery Health

Over time, all laptop batteries lose capacity. Knowing your battery health helps you plan.

**Generate a battery report:**
1. Click Start, type **"cmd"**, right-click Command Prompt > **"Run as administrator"**
2. Type this and press Enter: **powercfg /batteryreport**
3. Open the report at: C:\Users\YourName\battery-report.html
4. Look for "Design Capacity" vs "Full Charge Capacity"
5. If Full Charge is much lower than Design, your battery has aged

**What the numbers mean:**
- 80-100% of original: Battery is healthy
- 60-80% of original: Battery is aging — you will notice shorter life
- Below 60% of original: Battery needs replacement soon

## Quick Battery-Saving Checklist

Before unplugging for a long period, do this 30-second check:

- Lower screen brightness to 50% or less
- Turn on Battery Saver
- Close apps you are not using
- Close extra browser tabs (keep 5 or fewer)
- Turn off Wi-Fi and Bluetooth if not needed
- Switch to "Best power efficiency" mode

---

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- Your battery life is under 1 hour on a full charge (may need replacement)
- The battery report shows below 50% of original capacity
- Your laptop only works when plugged in
- The battery is swollen or the laptop case is bulging (safety hazard — stop using immediately)
- The laptop shuts down suddenly even when the battery shows charge remaining

## FAQ

**Q: Should I keep my laptop plugged in all the time?**
**A:** No. Keeping the battery at 100% constantly ages it faster. If you use your laptop as a desktop, unplug it occasionally and let the battery drain to 40-50% before recharging.

**Q: Is it bad to use my laptop while it charges?**
**A:** No, this is perfectly fine. Modern laptops are designed to be used while charging.

**Q: How long should a laptop battery last?**
**A:** Most laptop batteries last 2-4 years or 300-500 charge cycles before noticeable degradation. If your laptop is older and battery life is poor, replacement is the only fix.
