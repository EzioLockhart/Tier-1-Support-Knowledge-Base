## Before You Call the Helpdesk

A slow browser is usually caused by too much stored data, too many open tabs, or outdated software. These fixes take a few minutes and do not require technical knowledge.

## Fix 1: Clear Your Browser Cache and Cookies

Your browser saves temporary files to load websites faster. Over time, these files build up and can slow everything down.

**Choose your browser:**

**Google Chrome:**
1. Click the **three dots (...)** in the top-right corner
2. Click **"Delete browsing data"** or **"Clear browsing data"**
3. Set "Time range" to **"All time"**
4. Check these boxes:
   - ✅ Cookies and other site data
   - ✅ Cached images and files
5. Leave other boxes unchecked
6. Click **"Delete data"**
7. Close Chrome completely and reopen it

**Microsoft Edge:**
1. Click the **three dots (...)** in the top-right corner
2. Click **"Settings"**
3. Click **"Privacy, search, and services"** on the left
4. Under "Clear browsing data", click **"Choose what to clear"**
5. Set "Time range" to **"All time"**
6. Check these boxes:
   - ✅ Cookies and other site data
   - ✅ Cached images and files
7. Click **"Clear now"**
8. Close Edge completely and reopen it

**Mozilla Firefox:**
1. Click the **three lines (☰)** in the top-right corner
2. Click **"Settings"**
3. Click **"Privacy & Security"** on the left
4. Scroll to "Cookies and Site Data"
5. Click **"Clear Data"**
6. Check both boxes:
   - ✅ Cookies and Site Data
   - ✅ Cached Web Content
7. Click **"Clear"**
8. Close Firefox completely and reopen it

**What to expect:** After clearing cache and cookies, websites may load slightly slower the first time you visit them. You will need to log back into most websites.

## Fix 2: Check for Browser Updates

An outdated browser can be slow and less secure.

**Google Chrome:**
1. Click the three dots (...) > **"Help"** > **"About Google Chrome"**
2. Chrome will automatically check for updates
3. If an update is available, click **"Relaunch"** to install it

**Microsoft Edge:**
1. Click the three dots (...) > **"Help and feedback"** > **"About Microsoft Edge"**
2. Edge will automatically check for updates
3. If an update is available, it will install automatically — restart Edge

**Mozilla Firefox:**
1. Click the three lines (☰) > **"Help"** > **"About Firefox"**
2. Firefox will automatically check for updates
3. If an update is available, click **"Restart to update Firefox"**

## Fix 3: Close Unnecessary Tabs

Each open tab uses memory. Too many tabs slow your browser and your computer.

1. Look at your open tabs at the top of the browser
2. Close any tabs you are not actively using:
   - Click the **X** on each tab
   - Or right-click a tab and select **"Close tab"**
3. Bookmark pages you want to keep for later:
   - Click the **star (☆)** in the address bar
   - Or press **Ctrl + D**
4. Try to keep open tabs to **10 or fewer** for best performance

**Quick tip:** Press **Ctrl + H** to see your browsing history and find pages you closed by accident.

---

## Fix 4: Disable Unnecessary Extensions

Extensions are small add-ons that run inside your browser. Too many extensions slow things down.

**Google Chrome & Microsoft Edge:**
1. Click the three dots (...) > **"Extensions"** (Chrome) or **"Extensions"** (Edge)
2. You will see a list of all installed extensions
3. Turn OFF any extension you do not use or recognize:
   - Click the blue toggle switch to turn it off
4. To completely remove an extension, click **"Remove"**

**Mozilla Firefox:**
1. Click the three lines (☰) > **"Add-ons and themes"**
2. Click **"Extensions"** on the left
3. Click the three dots next to any extension > **"Disable"** or **"Remove"**

**Which extensions to keep:** Only keep extensions you installed intentionally and use regularly. If you are unsure what an extension does, disable it.

---

## Fix 5: Restart Your Computer

Sometimes the problem is not the browser — it is the computer.

1. Close all open programs, including your browser
2. Click the Start button (Windows icon)
3. Click the Power button
4. Select **"Restart"**
5. Wait for the computer to restart
6. Open only your browser and test

**Why this helps:** Restarting clears temporary memory and background processes that can slow your browser even when you are not running many programs.

---

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- Your browser is still slow after trying all five fixes
- Websites are blocked or show security warnings
- Your browser keeps redirecting to strange websites (possible malware)
- You see pop-ups even when not browsing (possible adware)
- Multiple browsers on your computer are all slow

---

## Prevention Tips

- Clear your cache and cookies once a month
- Close tabs you are not using — bookmark them instead
- Only install extensions from trusted sources
- Keep your browser updated (most update automatically)
- Restart your computer at least once a week
