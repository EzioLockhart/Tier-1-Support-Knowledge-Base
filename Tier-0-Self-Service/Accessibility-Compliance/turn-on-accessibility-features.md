## About Windows Accessibility Features

Windows has built-in tools to make your computer easier to use. Whether you need larger text, screen reading, captions, or keyboard assistance, these features are free and ready to use.

## Tool 1: Magnifier — Enlarge Part of Your Screen

Magnifier zooms in on any part of your screen.

**How to turn it on:**
1. Press **Windows key + Plus sign (+)** on your keyboard
2. The screen zooms in
3. Press **Windows key + Plus (+)** again to zoom in more
4. Press **Windows key + Minus sign (-)** to zoom out
5. Press **Windows key + Esc** to turn Magnifier off

**How to adjust Magnifier settings:**
1. Click Start > Settings > **"Accessibility"** > **"Magnifier"**
2. Choose your zoom level (100% to 1600%)
3. Choose your view:
   - **Full screen:** Entire screen is magnified
   - **Lens:** Only the area around your mouse is magnified
   - **Docked:** A fixed area at the top of the screen shows the magnified view

**Quick tip:** You can also turn Magnifier on and off from Settings > Accessibility > Magnifier > toggle ON or OFF.

---

## Tool 2: Narrator — Have Your Screen Read Aloud

Narrator reads text, buttons, and menus aloud.

**How to turn it on:**
1. Press **Windows key + Ctrl + Enter**
2. Narrator starts reading immediately
3. Press the same keys again to turn it off

**How to adjust Narrator settings:**
1. Click Start > Settings > **"Accessibility"** > **"Narrator"**
2. Choose a voice — different voices are available
3. Adjust the speed and pitch of the voice
4. Choose how much detail you want Narrator to read

**Useful Narrator shortcuts:**
- **Caps Lock + R:** Start reading from current position
- **Caps Lock + H:** Jump to next heading
- **Ctrl:** Stop reading
- **Caps Lock + F1:** Show all Narrator commands

---

## Tool 3: Live Captions — See Words for All Audio

Live Captions automatically creates captions for any audio playing on your computer — videos, meetings, podcasts.

**How to turn it on:**
1. Press **Windows key + Ctrl + L**
2. A caption bar appears at the top or bottom of your screen
3. Captions appear automatically whenever audio plays
4. Press the same keys to turn it off

**How to adjust Live Captions:**
1. Click the settings gear on the caption bar
2. Choose caption position (top or bottom of screen)
3. Adjust the caption style (colors, size, background)

**What it works with:** YouTube videos, Teams meetings, Zoom calls, video players, and any audio from your browser or apps.

---

## Tool 4: High Contrast — Make Everything Easier to See

High Contrast changes the colors on your screen to make text and buttons stand out clearly.

**How to turn it on:**
1. Press **Left Alt + Left Shift + Print Screen**
2. Click **"Yes"** to confirm
3. The screen colors change to a high-contrast theme
4. Press the same keys to turn it off

**How to adjust High Contrast:**
1. Click Start > Settings > **"Accessibility"** > **"Contrast themes"**
2. Choose a theme:
   - Aquatic (blue tones)
   - Desert (warm tones)
   - Dusk (dark with bright text)
   - Night sky (black with white text)
3. Click the theme to apply it
4. You can also create your own custom contrast theme

---

## Tool 5: Make Text Larger Without Changing Screen Resolution

You can increase text size across Windows without affecting screen sharpness.

**How to adjust text size:**
1. Click Start > Settings > **"Accessibility"** > **"Text size"**
2. Use the slider to increase text size
3. The preview shows how text will look
4. Click **"Apply"**
5. Text across Windows, menus, and supported apps will be larger

**Note:** This only affects text in Windows and supported apps. It does not change the size of images or videos.

---

## Tool 6: Sticky Keys and Filter Keys — Keyboard Assistance

These features help if you have difficulty pressing multiple keys at once or pressing keys lightly.

**Sticky Keys:** Lets you press one key at a time for shortcuts.
1. Press **Shift five times quickly**
2. Click **"Yes"** to turn on Sticky Keys
3. Now you can press **Ctrl**, then **Alt**, then **Delete** one at a time instead of all at once

**Filter Keys:** Ignores brief or repeated keystrokes.
1. Hold the **right Shift key for 8 seconds**
2. Click **"Yes"** to turn on Filter Keys
3. Windows now ignores very short key presses

**How to adjust keyboard settings:**
1. Click Start > Settings > **"Accessibility"** > **"Keyboard"**
2. Turn Sticky Keys, Filter Keys, or Toggle Keys ON or OFF
3. Adjust settings for each feature

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- An accessibility feature does not turn on using the keyboard shortcut
- You need an accessibility feature that requires administrator setup
- You need assistive technology software not built into Windows
- Accessibility features interfere with your work applications
- You need help choosing which features best suit your needs

## FAQ

**Q: Are accessibility features available on all Windows computers?**
**A:** Yes. Magnifier, Narrator, Live Captions, High Contrast, and keyboard assistance are built into every Windows 10 and Windows 11 computer. No additional software or downloads are needed.

**Q: Will turning on accessibility features slow down my computer?**
**A:** No. These features are designed to be lightweight and should not noticeably affect performance. Live Captions uses a small amount of processing power but is optimized to run efficiently.

**Q: Can I use more than one accessibility feature at the same time?**
**A:** Yes. For example, you can use Magnifier and Narrator together, or High Contrast with larger text. Most features work well together.
