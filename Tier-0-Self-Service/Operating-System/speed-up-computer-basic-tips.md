## Tip 1: Restart Your Computer

This is the simplest and most effective fix for a slow computer.

**How to do it properly:**
1. Click the **Start button** (Windows icon)
2. Click the **Power button**
3. Select **"Restart"** — not "Shut down"
4. Wait for the computer to restart completely
5. Log in and test if it feels faster

**Why this works:** Restarting clears temporary files, stops stuck background programs, and gives your computer a fresh start. If you only use "Sleep" or close the lid, your computer never fully resets.

**How often:** Restart at least once a week. Many people go weeks or months without restarting.

**What to tell yourself:** "Restarting is like a good night's sleep for my computer. It clears out all the temporary junk and starts fresh."

## Tip 2: Close Programs You Are Not Using

Every open program uses memory. Too many open programs slow everything down.

**How to see what is running:**
1. Look at your **taskbar** at the bottom of the screen
2. Each icon with a line under it is an open program
3. Count how many are open — more than 5-7 is a lot

**What to close:**
1. Right-click each program icon you are not using
2. Select **"Close window"**
3. Pay special attention to:
   - Web browsers (each tab uses memory)
   - Video conferencing apps (Teams, Zoom, Skype)
   - Music and video apps
   - Large programs (Photoshop, Excel with large files)

**Quick check with Task Manager:**
1. Press **Ctrl + Shift + Esc** to open Task Manager
2. Look at the **"Processes"** tab
3. Sort by Memory by clicking the "Memory" column header
4. See which programs use the most memory
5. Close the ones you do not need

**What to tell yourself:** "My computer only has so much memory. If too many programs are open, there is not enough to go around. Closing unused programs frees up memory for the ones I am actually using."

## Tip 3: Stop Programs from Launching at Startup

Many programs automatically start when your computer turns on, slowing down boot time.

**How to disable startup programs:**
1. Press **Ctrl + Shift + Esc** to open Task Manager
2. Click the **"Startup"** tab (left sidebar in Windows 11, top tab in Windows 10)
3. You will see a list of programs with their "Startup impact"
4. Look for programs marked **"High"** impact
5. Right-click any program you do not need at startup > **"Disable"**

**Which programs are safe to disable:**
- Spotify, Apple Music, or other music apps
- Adobe Creative Cloud or updaters
- Steam or gaming software
- Skype, Slack, or messaging apps
- OneDrive (if you do not use it)
- Browser toolbars and updaters

**Which programs to KEEP enabled:**
- Antivirus or security software
- Audio or graphics drivers
- Programs from your computer manufacturer that you need
- Anything from Microsoft that you are unsure about

**What to tell yourself:** "Every program that starts with Windows slows down boot time. I will disable the ones I can open manually when I need them."

## Tip 4: Free Up Disk Space

When your hard drive is nearly full, your computer slows down significantly.

**Step 1: Check how much free space you have**
1. Open **File Explorer** (folder icon)
2. Click **"This PC"** on the left
3. Look at your C: drive — the bar shows how full it is
4. If the bar is **red** or shows less than 10 GB free, your drive is too full

**Step 2: Delete temporary files**
1. Click Start > **Settings** (gear icon) > **System** > **Storage**
2. Click **"Temporary files"**
3. Check all boxes
4. Click **"Remove files"**

**Step 3: Empty your Recycle Bin**
1. Right-click the **Recycle Bin** icon on your desktop
2. Select **"Empty Recycle Bin"**
3. Click **"Yes"** to confirm

**Step 4: Uninstall programs you no longer use**
1. Go to Settings > Apps > Installed apps
2. Sort by **"Size"** to see the largest programs
3. Click the three dots (...) next to any program you do not need
4. Select **"Uninstall"**

**Step 5: Move large files to cloud storage**
1. Move personal videos, photos, and documents to OneDrive, Google Drive, or an external drive
2. These files take up lots of space and can be stored elsewhere

**What to tell yourself:** "My hard drive is like a filing cabinet. When it's completely full, finding anything takes forever. Freeing up space makes everything faster."

## Tip 5: Check for Windows Updates

Updates often include performance improvements and bug fixes.

**How to check:**
1. Click Start > **Settings** (gear icon)
2. Click **"Windows Update"** (Windows 11) or **"Update & Security"** (Windows 10)
3. Click **"Check for updates"**
4. If updates are available, click **"Install now"**
5. Restart your computer when prompted

**Why this helps:** Updates fix bugs that may be slowing your computer. They also include security improvements that protect against malware that can secretly slow things down.

**What to tell yourself:** "Windows updates are like tune-ups for my computer. They fix problems I might not even know about."

## Tip 6: Reduce Visual Effects

Windows animations and shadows look nice but use processing power.

**How to adjust for better performance:**
1. Press **Windows key + R**, type **"sysdm.cpl"**, press Enter
2. Go to the **"Advanced"** tab
3. Under "Performance", click **"Settings"**
4. Select **"Adjust for best performance"** for maximum speed, or
5. Select **"Custom"** and uncheck these:
   - Animate windows when minimizing and maximizing
   - Animations in the taskbar
   - Fade or slide menus into view
   - Show shadows under windows
6. Keep checked: **"Smooth edges of screen fonts"** (this makes text readable)
7. Click **"Apply"** and **"OK"**

**What to tell yourself:** "Animations and shadows look nice but use processing power. Turning them off makes my computer feel snappier, especially on older machines."

## Quick Performance Checklist

When your computer feels slow, do this 5-minute check:

- [ ] Restart your computer
- [ ] Close programs and browser tabs you are not using
- [ ] Open Task Manager > Startup tab > disable high-impact programs
- [ ] Check your C: drive has at least 10 GB free
- [ ] Run Windows Update

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- Your computer is still very slow after trying all tips
- Task Manager shows disk usage stuck at 100% constantly
- Your computer takes more than 5 minutes to boot
- You hear clicking or grinding sounds from your computer
- Programs crash or freeze frequently
- You suspect your computer has a virus

## FAQ

**Q: How often should I restart my computer?**
**A:** At least once a week. More often if you notice it slowing down. Restarting clears temporary issues that build up over time.

**Q: Will deleting files make my computer faster?**
**A:** Only if your hard drive is nearly full. If you have plenty of free space (more than 20 GB), deleting files will not noticeably improve speed. Focus on closing programs and disabling startup items instead.

**Q: Why is my new computer slow?**
**A:** New computers often come with many pre-installed programs (bloatware) that run at startup. Check the Startup tab in Task Manager and disable anything you do not need. Also check for Windows updates — new computers may need several rounds of updates.
