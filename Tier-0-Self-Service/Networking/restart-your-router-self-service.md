## Symptoms

- Internet is slow or not working on all devices
- Wi-Fi network name not appearing in available networks
- Websites not loading despite Wi-Fi showing as connected
- Router lights blinking unusually or showing red/orange
- Internet works briefly then disconnects
- Video calls keep freezing or dropping
- You have already restarted your computer but internet still does not work

---

## Before You Start

- Restarting your router is safe and will not change your Wi-Fi password or network name
- This process takes about 5 minutes
- All devices in your home will temporarily lose internet connection
- No technical skills required

---

## Symptom-to-Action Mapping

| If You See This | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| Internet not working on all devices | Restart both modem and router |
| Wi-Fi network not appearing | Restart router only |
| Internet slow but working | Restart router first, then modem if needed |
| Router lights all off | Check power cable, then restart |
| Issue started after a power outage | Restart both devices |

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Identify Your Equipment
1. Look at your internet setup — you may have one or two devices
2. A modem connects to the wall (cable or phone line) and brings internet into your home
3. A router creates your Wi-Fi network — it has antennas
4. Some homes have a combined modem-router (one device that does both)
5. Identify which device you have before proceeding

### Step 2: Unplug Both Devices
1. If you have separate modem and router:
   - Unplug the power cable from the router first
   - Unplug the power cable from the modem second
2. If you have a combined modem-router:
   - Unplug the power cable from the single device
3. Leave everything unplugged for 60 full seconds
   - This allows the devices to fully discharge and clear their memory
   - Do not skip the waiting time — it is essential

### Step 3: Plug Everything Back In
1. Plug in the modem first (the device connected to the wall)
2. Wait for the modem lights to stabilize — typically 1-2 minutes
   - Look for solid green lights (not blinking rapidly)
3. Plug in the router second (the device with antennas)
4. Wait for the router lights to stabilize — typically 1-2 minutes
   - The Wi-Fi light should be solid or blinking normally
5. If you have a combined modem-router, simply plug it in and wait 2-3 minutes

### Step 4: Test Your Connection
1. On your computer or phone, look for your Wi-Fi network
2. Connect to your Wi-Fi network
3. Open a web browser and go to any website (e.g., google.com)
4. If the website loads, your internet is working
5. If still not working after 5 minutes, restart your computer as well

## What to Do If Restart Does Not Work

Try these additional steps:

- Check if other devices in your home can connect — if only one device fails, the problem is with that device
- Check for an internet outage in your area:
  - Visit your ISP's website from your phone (using cellular data)
  - Check downdetector.com for reported outages
- Contact your Internet Service Provider if:
  - Restarting does not help
  - Router lights show red or are off after restart
  - Multiple neighbors report the same issue

## When Restarting Typically Fixes the Problem

| Issue | Why It Works |
|-------|--------------|
| Slow internet | Clears router memory that fills up over time |
| Wi-Fi not appearing | Resets the Wi-Fi radio that may have crashed |
| Intermittent drops | Re-establishes a clean connection with your ISP |
| After power outages | Allows the modem to re-sync with the ISP network |
| Router running hot | Gives the device time to cool down |

## Prevention Tips

- Restart your router once a month to keep it running smoothly
- Keep your router in an open, ventilated area — not inside a cabinet
- Do not stack other devices on top of your router
- If you frequently need to restart, your router may be old and need replacement (typically every 3-5 years)
- Keep your router away from heat sources and direct sunlight

## FAQ

**Q: How often should I restart my router?**
**A:** Once a month is recommended. If you find yourself restarting weekly or daily, the router may be failing and should be replaced.

**Q: Will restarting my router delete my Wi-Fi password?**
**A:** No. All your settings, including Wi-Fi name and password, are saved permanently and will not change when you restart.

**Q: What is the difference between restarting and resetting?**
**A:** Restarting (power off and on) just reboots the device. Resetting (pressing a small button with a paperclip) erases all settings and returns the router to factory defaults. Never reset your router unless instructed by your ISP.
