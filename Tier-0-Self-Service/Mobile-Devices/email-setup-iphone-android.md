## Before You Start

**What you need:**
- Your smartphone (iPhone or Android)
- Your work email address and password
- About 5-10 minutes of time

**Which method to use:** We recommend using the Microsoft Outlook app for the best experience. It includes your email, calendar, and contacts all in one place.

## Step 1: Download Microsoft Outlook

**On iPhone:**
1. Open the **App Store**
2. Tap the **Search** tab at the bottom
3. Type "Microsoft Outlook" in the search bar
4. Look for the app with the blue envelope icon by Microsoft Corporation
5. Tap **"Get"** and wait for it to download
6. Tap **"Open"** when it is ready

**On Android:**
1. Open the **Google Play Store**
2. Tap the **Search** bar at the top
3. Type "Microsoft Outlook" 
4. Look for the app with the blue envelope icon by Microsoft Corporation
5. Tap **"Install"** and wait for it to download
6. Tap **"Open"** when it is ready

**What to tell yourself:** "The Outlook app is free. I'm looking for the official Microsoft logo — a blue envelope."

---

## Step 2: Add Your Work Email Account

1. When Outlook opens for the first time, you will see "Add Account"
2. Enter your **full work email address**
   - Example: john.doe@yourcompany.com
3. Tap **"Continue"** or **"Add Account"**
4. Enter your **password** when prompted
5. If your organization uses Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):
   - You will receive a verification prompt
   - Approve it using your authenticator app, SMS code, or phone call
6. Outlook will set up your account automatically — this takes about 30 seconds
7. When setup is complete, you will see your inbox

**If you already have Outlook installed and want to add another account:**
1. Open Outlook
2. Tap your profile icon (top-left corner)
3. Tap the **gear icon (⚙️)** > **"Add Account"**
4. Follow the same steps above

**What to tell yourself:** "If my organization uses MFA, I need my phone nearby to approve the sign-in. The app sets up everything automatically."

---

## Step 3: Enable Notifications

Make sure you get alerted when new emails arrive.

**On iPhone:**
1. Go to **Settings** (the gear icon on your home screen)
2. Scroll down and tap **"Outlook"**
3. Tap **"Notifications"**
4. Turn **"Allow Notifications"** ON (green)
5. Check "Lock Screen", "Notification Center", and "Banners" are all selected

**On Android:**
1. Go to **Settings** > **"Apps"** > **"Outlook"**
2. Tap **"Notifications"**
3. Turn **"Show notifications"** ON
4. Tap "Mail" and ensure notifications are enabled

**In the Outlook app itself:**
1. Open Outlook
2. Tap your profile icon > gear icon (⚙️)
3. Tap **"Notifications"**
4. Enable notifications for new mail

**What to tell yourself:** "I want to know when important emails arrive. I'll check that notifications are on both in my phone settings and in the Outlook app."

## Step 5: Basic Troubleshooting

**If emails are not showing up:**
1. Make sure your phone has internet — open Safari or Chrome and go to google.com
2. In Outlook, pull down from the top of the inbox to refresh
3. Check if Airplane Mode is accidentally on (swipe down from top of screen)

**If Outlook says your password is wrong:**
1. Make sure you are entering your current work password
2. Check that Caps Lock is not on (passwords are case-sensitive)
3. If you recently changed your password, you may need to update it in the app
   - In Outlook, tap profile icon > gear icon > your account > "Delete Account"
   - Then add the account again with your new password

**If you are not getting notifications:**
1. Check that notifications are enabled (Step 3)
2. Check that Do Not Disturb or Focus mode is not on
3. Restart your phone

## When to Call the Helpdesk

Contact IT support if:

- You cannot sign in after multiple attempts
- Outlook says "Account not found" or "Access denied"
- You cannot install apps on your phone (may be restricted by company policy)
- Your account locks after attempting to sign in
- Emails still do not sync after trying all troubleshooting steps

## FAQ

**Q: Should I use the Outlook app or my phone's built-in Mail app?**
**A:** We recommend the Outlook app. It works best with work accounts, includes your calendar and contacts, and gets new features first. The built-in Mail app works but has fewer features.

**Q: Will my personal emails mix with my work emails?**
**A:** No. Each account has its own inbox. You can switch between accounts in the Outlook app by tapping your profile icon.

**Q: Does Outlook use a lot of my phone's data?**
**A:** No. Outlook only syncs email headers (subject and sender). Full emails and attachments load when you open them. Typical monthly data usage is very low.
